Gamma Waves

Space | Medicine | Citations

Gamma Waves are a form of electromagnetic wave, which consist of a extremely short wavelength (less than 1 tenth of a nanometer) which are highly energetic.

Electromagnetic Spectrum:

Image (1): Gamma Waves are depicted as the most energetic type of wave, consisting of extreme short frequency waves.

Properties of Gamma Waves

-Gamma Waves is one of the forms of radiation, being preceeded by Alpha and Beta Particle Radiation.
-Extremely similar to X-rays
-Electromagnetic Radiation, which means it has no mass.
-Capable of indirectly ionizing matter
-Easily pass through objects

Image (2): Movement of Gamma Waves, in comparison to other forms of radiation.

History

-Named by Ernest Rutherford
-Discovered by Paul Villard in 1905
-Discovered in Paris, was assisted by the research of Pierre and Marie Curie
-Was the third type of radioactive component found, being preceded by Alpha and Beta waves.

Important Dates

  • 1905– Villard discovers a third component of radioactivity, which become known as gamma rays, following the notation of Rutherford.
  • 1910– Bragg demonstrates that gamma rays ionize gas just like x-rays.
  • 1914-Rutherford & Andrade demonstrate the wave nature of gamma rays.
  • 1927- Compton defines the quantum of light as being the photon. X-rays and Gamma rays are found to be photons.

Production of Gamma Waves

There are 4 ways to produce Gamma Waves

  1. 1. A high-energy particle can collide with another particle

    2. Image (3): Two particles colliding with each other, causing the release of gamma waves.

  2. 2. A particle can collide and annihilate with its anti-particle

    3. Image (4): A particle and its antiparticle collide, releasing gamma waves.

  3. 3. An element can undergo radioactive decay

    4. Image (5): A radioactive isotope goes throgh decay, releasing energy in the form of gamma waves.

  4. 4. A charged particle can be accelerated

    5. Image (6): A magnetic field affects a charged particle, which leads to the release of gamma waves
